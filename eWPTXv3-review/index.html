Hello friend …..
I am back again with another writeup.

This time it’s about eWPTX v3 — the eLearnSecurity Web Penetration Testing eXtreme certification from INE / eLearnSecurity.

About the Exam

The eWPTX v3 exam is an 18-hour straight, hands-on exam.
Once the exam starts, you receive a Letter of Engagement (LoE) that clearly defines:

  • The scope of the penetration test
  • Allowed and disallowed actions
  • Targets and limitations
  • General rules and expectations

The exam contains 45 questions:

  • Some are theoretical
  • Others require hands-on exploitation and answers from the lab environment

Due to NDA restrictions, I can’t go into too many details about the actual lab, but I can share my experience and preparation approach.

My Experience

I wouldn’t say the exam was hard, but I also wouldn’t say it was trivial.
It sits in a very good place: challenging, realistic, and fair.

The difficulty is not about obscure tricks or CTF-style puzzles.
Instead, it focuses on:

  • Understanding web application logic
  • Finding real-world vulnerabilities
  • Chaining issues together
  • Thinking like an actual web penetration tester

Time-wise, 18 hours is more than enough if:

  • You are well-prepared
  • You stay organized
  • You avoid overthinking simple things

Honestly, I really, really enjoyed the exam.

Course Content Review

The content provided by Alexis Ahmed was spectacular — well-structured, deep, and extremely practical.

It doesn’t just teach you what vulnerabilities are, but:

  • Why they exist
  • How they happen in real applications
  • How to exploit them responsibly

I learned a lot, even though I already had solid web security experience.

That said, I did not rely on the course alone.

Additional Preparation

I didn’t stop at the official material.
I also skimmed through and practiced multiple PortSwigger Web Security Academy modules, especially focusing on areas that frequently appear in real-world assessments.

Most Important Topics for the Exam

These topics are critical for eWPTX v3:

  • SQL Injection
    • Classic
    • Blind
    • Error-based
  • API Security Testing
    • Authentication flaws
    • Broken object level authorization (BOLA)
  • JWT (JSON Web Tokens)
    • Weak secrets
    • Algorithm confusion
    • Token tampering
  • NoSQL Injection
  • Insecure Deserialization
  • Authentication & Authorization Logic
  • Business Logic Flaws

If you are weak in any of these areas, you will feel it during the exam.

Exam Environment & Flow

  • The exam starts immediately after receiving the Letter of Engagement
  • Everything is clearly scoped
  • The lab environment feels realistic, not artificial
  • Enumeration and methodology matter more than tools

This is not an automated-scanner exam.
Manual testing, understanding responses, and thinking logically are key.

Final Thoughts

To be honest:

  • The exam is pretty easy if you are well prepared
  • It is not designed to trick you
  • It truly tests whether you can perform real web penetration testing

I enjoyed every part of it — from enumeration to exploitation to answering the questions.

If you:

  • Completed the course seriously
  • Practiced on PortSwigger
  • Have real-world web testing experience

Then you are more than ready.

Would I Recommend eWPTX?

Absolutely.

eWPTX v3 is one of the best advanced web security certifications out there.
It focuses on real skills, not memorization.

If your goal is to become a strong web penetration tester, this exam is 100% worth it.

Good luck to everyone planning to take it — and always remember to “TRY HARDER”
